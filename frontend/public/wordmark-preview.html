V1 · Bhav | X · EXCHANGE (ticker bar + caption)

Vertical gold bar separates Bhav and X. Tiny "EXCHANGE" caption under X explicitly defines what X stands for. NSE/BSE ticker-tape DNA. Bhav is the brand, X is the exchange — each owns its space.

V2 · Bhav — X with tagline "INDIA'S METAL EXCHANGE"

Em-dash separator between Bhav and X (more elegant than vertical bar). Below the wordmark: small caps tagline that defines the entire identity. The two parts are visually separate but unified by the line beneath. Most editorial, most premium.

V3 · Bhav · ⟨X⟩ (X in thin gold frame)

X gets its own gold-bordered frame — a thin metallic outline around the letter. Looks like a stock chip / ticker badge. Bhav stays naked serif. The frame visually elevates X to its own identity — "this is the exchange marker, not just a letter."

BhavX

V4 · Bhav X with descriptor underline

Wide letter-spacing between Bhav and X — pure spatial separation. Below: a thin descriptor flanked by gold lines. Cleanest, most timeless. The space between Bhav and X is itself meaningful — like a gap on a temple inscription.

BhavX Bharat's Metal Exchange

V5 · Bhav + [X] solid-gold ticker chip

X becomes a solid gold chip — like a stock symbol badge or premium watch hour-marker. Sans-serif Inter inside the chip (contrasts with Marcellus serif of "Bhav"). Bhav is the brand identity, X is the tradeable instrument. Maximum separation, maximum exchange feel.

V6 · Bhav XE (X with tiny exchange superscript)

Minimalist abbreviation. X has a tiny "E" superscript — like ™ position but for "Exchange". Most compact. Reads as "Bhav X-Exchange". Subtle but defines the X without overloading the wordmark.
